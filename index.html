About Us

CAMPMUN is a premier, weeklong educational program designed for students interested in global affairs and diplomacy. The camp provides an immersive experience that enables students to develop their negotiation, communication, and leadership skills while exploring the most pressing global challenges facing the world today. Through simulations of real United Nations meetings, students gain a deeper understanding of international relations, learn to engage in constructive dialogue, and develop creative solutions to complex problems.

After going through CAMPMUN, our team can assure students will:

  • Foster Leadership and Diplomacy Skills
  • Enhance Critical Thinking and Communication Skills
  • Develop Global Awareness and Understanding

CAMPMUN runs 6 days, monday through friday being virtual half-day classes teaching all neccessary Model UN skills and saturday being the in-person conference date. For a more in-depth time frame, check the schedule tab. Sign up today for an fun yet educational experience, because learning today brings leading tommorow.

Frequently Asked Questions

List of frequently asked and important questions regarding CAMPMUN II

  • 01 Is CAMPMUN II going to be held in-person?

    For the first five days (Monday - Friday), CAMPMUN will be entirely virtual, with 1 - 2 hourlong lessons alongside coursework assigned for outside of class; however, on the last day of CAMPMUN (Saturday), we will require participants to gather in-person to practice their newly learning skills through a Model UN competition. This should last most of the day and lunch needs to be packed and brought. More in-depth planning can be found in the schedule tab.

  • 02 Where will the in-person CAMPMUN II conference be held?

    The conference on Saturday, July 15th will be held at Green Acres Community Center. Specific information will be available in the Google Classroom once registered.

  • 03 Who will be teaching the camp?

    CAMPMUN II will be taught by Heman Bekele, Jamie Kim, Shreyas Jain, and Dina Kurdi, 4 national award winning Model UN participants. Jamie and Dina are both active participants in Thomas Jefferson High School for Science and Technology's Model United Nations team, and Shreyas and Heman are currently captains of the Frost Middle School Model UN team. For a more in depth profile of every leader, check the team tab.

  • 04 How much will CAMPMUN cost to attend?

    For only 35 dollars, students will get access to a weeklong summer camp taught by experienced professionals. This will be paid when registering and there will be no refunds.
